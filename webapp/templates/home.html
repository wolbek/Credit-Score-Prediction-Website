What is credit score?

A credit score is a number from 300 to 850 that rates a consumer’s creditworthiness. The higher the score, the better a borrower looks to potential lenders. A credit score is based on credit history: - number of open accounts total levels of debt repayment history, and other factors.

Why you should calculate it?

Companies use credit scores to make decisions on whether to offer you a mortgage, credit card, auto loan, and also for tenant screening and insurance. They are also used to determine the interest rate and credit limit you receive. Having a good credit score gives you leverage while applying for a loan.

What can damage your credit score?

Making a late payment, having a high debt to credit utilization ratio, applying for a lot of credit at once, closing a credit card account or stopping your credit-related activities for an extended period can damange your credit score.
